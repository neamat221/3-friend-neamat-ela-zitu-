Zitu

Zitu’s day didn’t start with an alarm—it started with tea. Strong tea, extra ginger, and no sugar debate allowed. Everyone in the area knew one thing: if Zitu hadn’t had her morning cha, it was safer to stay quiet. She believed tea could solve any problem. Tension? Cha. Sad mood? Cha. Big decision? Two cups of cha. Her favorite place was the roadside tea stall, where she discussed life, politics, and drama with the tea seller like a professor. “এগুলো পড়তে হয় না”
